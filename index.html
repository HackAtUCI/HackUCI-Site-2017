FAQ

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is a place where the only thing limiting what you build is your own creativity - we'll provide the rest. Stay up for 36 hours with the smartest people you'll ever meet, building the coolest things you have created, and chugging more of your favorite energy drink than you ever thought possible.

Who can come?

Any undergraduate or graduate students. We also admit high school students on a case-by-case basis.

What should I bring?

Yourself, deodorant, an idea, a photo ID, your hacking setup, a change of clothes, toiletries, and optionally a sleeping bag (pillow and/or blanket). Note: There will be no showers at the event.

What should I create?

You can build whatever you can dream of, whether that be a website with an amazing feature, a phone app, or a rad piece of hardware (Soldering is NOT allowed)! Our only rule is that all code must be written during the hackathon itself. Feel free to use open source libraries and APIs; companies attending may provide their own as well. Impress us!

When does the event end?

The closing ceremony will end at 4pm on Sunday.

Do I sign up as a team or individual?

You don't sign up as a team. When you submit your hack at the end of the hacking period, you can include all your beautiful teammates. You can have a team of up to five members, or go at it alone! If you're looking for team members, we'll help you connect to talented participants before hacking begins.

I live far away. How do I get to the event?

Buses will be provided for UCI students only. Students from other schools are encouraged to carpool to the event. Additional parking information will be available soon.

How will hacks be presented?

On Sunday at 1:00pm, all hackers will have the option to demo their hacks to sponsors and their fellow hackers in an expo event.

Why are there no general prizes?

We believe in hacking for the sake of hacking; doing cool and challenging things because they are rewarding in their own sense. Prizes discourage collaboration and encourage competition; this is not the type of culture we wish to promote. Instead, we invested the saved funds into making the event even better for everyone! However, our sponsors may offer a prize based on their criteria.
